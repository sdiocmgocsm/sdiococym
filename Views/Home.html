Rev. Fr. Wilson Mathew

The Dean for this year’s conference will be Rev. Fr. Wilson Mathew, a member of Basil Dayara, Pathanamthitta (Thumpamon Diocese).

Fr.Mathew has the ability and willingness to talk about our faith. Recognizes the importance of the Church and the role of the Church as teacher. Has shown the capacity and evidence of living a chaste celibate life. Giving leadership to several family conferences, conventions, youth meetings, counseling groups around several dioceses of our Church. He is a faculty of the Orthodox Theological Seminary, Kottayam(Kerala).

We are grateful for the years the Lord has allowed us to meet together for the Family & Youth Conference. We owe much to Him for the word and fellowship from these times that has shown us our Immeasurable Christ and His Great Purpose. Because of the way He has used these opportunities, we have grown in the Lord and have been built together in Him. We believe we are to proceed with a 2017 conference. We realize that we are living in a most critical hour just prior to the Lord’s return. What are we to do?

The burden that we believe that is on the Lord’s heart for this hour is “BE TRANSFORMED” Transfer and Transfiguration are stages of Transform –Process. The present world is in “a growing strategy.” But, “a transformed growth”, is an in-evitable process of the whole development. A spiritual transformation of ‘Self’ is the corner stone of family, society, church – and the entire world. “BE TRANSFORMED” is a call and an advice to our community. It is a personal response, resource and a resistance.

The current generation that is carrying the testimony must press on remaining faithful to what we have seen and seeing to it that we have a bright burning testimony to pass on. That impacts our personal and corporate lives as we press on to being conformed to his image and in strengthening His body. This generation must take up their responsibility to “BE TRANSFORMED” and to serve God’s Eternal Purpose in their generation.
